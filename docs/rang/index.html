Rang is Court Piece

Rang (also spelled Rung) is the Punjabi, Urdu, and Gujarati name for what English speakers often call Court Piece or Trump. Four players, two teams, trick-taking with a bid and a trump suit. The scoring model is identical — so the app handles both names the same way.

How Rang scoring works

Each round has a bidder who names trump. The bidder's team tries to win at least the bid number of tricks; the opposition tries to block them. Win: bidder's side gains +bid points per player, opposition loses the same. Loss: signs flip. Optional bidder bonus applies only to the bidder.

Black Queen Scorer recomputes totals from scratch every time a round changes, so editing or deleting a round never leaves stale numbers on the scoreboard.

Card-night flow

Start a session in seconds — add 4 to 12 players, lock the seating order that matches your table, pick the bidder bonus. From there, every round is four taps: bidder, teammate, bid, Won or Lost. The round history is editable; the leaderboard reorders with a smooth animation as scores move.

Live-share the scoreboard

Tap broadcast on any session to generate a QR code. Anyone scans it and watches your Rang scoreboard update in real time in any browser. No app install or sign-up for viewers.
